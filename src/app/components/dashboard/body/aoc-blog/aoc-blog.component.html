Organic cultivation of cardamom

August 10, 2022 by Akhl VB

In organic cultivation of cardamom, the methods to be followed should conform to the standards laid down for the purpose.

  • Manuring - Application of organic manures such as neem cake @ 1 kg or poultry manure/farmyard manure/compost/vermicompost @ 2kg per plant may be done once in a year during May-June. Application of Mussorie rock phosphate or bone meal may be done, if found necessary, based on soil analysis.
  • Diseases - The major fungal diseases affecting cardamom are azhukal (Phytophthora medii) and clump rot (Pythium vexans, Rhizoctonia solani and Fusarium sp.). Incorporation of Trichoderma multiplied in suitable organic medium in the plant base (1 kg per clump) prior to the onset of monsoon season (May) is a prophylactic operation for clump rot disease. Use of Bordeaux mixture 1 % when found necessary may be resorted to. Regular rouging of virus affected plants should be made to reduce the spread. Rouged plants should be destroyed by burning.
  • Bacterial and fungal biofertilizers - Bacterial and fungal biofertilizers are used as a component for our organic farming in different crops.
  • Inspect pest management - The main strategy to combat harmful pests is to build up a population of beneficial insects, whose larvae feed off the eggs of pests.
  • Weed management - In our organic farming, chemical herbicides are not using so only manual weeding.

Usually, the cardamom plant requires 35 to 40 months to produce capsules containing seeds.
